01 / The real mandate

Design quality upstream, where it is still cheap to change.

Braintrust's AI-lab client is not hiring an operations manager to push tasks through a queue. It needs a senior thought partner who can turn research and business intent into a defensible data-and-evaluation design before contributor scale, tooling, and pilot execution begin.

01
Speed to pilot

must not outrun measurement validity. A fast pilot built on a weak construct creates confident noise.

02
Benchmark consistency

must coexist with task context. Reusable methodology matters, but not at the cost of flattening legitimate domain variation.

03
Human judgment at scale

must preserve expertise and ambiguity. Ground truth can be authoritative without pretending every difficult case has one obvious answer.

04
Advisory influence

must work without operational command. The strategist earns leverage by clarifying decisions, exposing failure modes, and giving program managers a stronger design.

02 / Evaluation Design Contract

Before data collection, agree on what the evidence must prove.

The contract is not a template for paperwork. It is a compact decision architecture that forces the project to resolve six design questions before scale.

Six clauses. One shared definition of good.

The quality system begins when researchers, engineering, program leadership, and human evaluators can point to the same intended behavior, evidence standard, and escalation path.

Downloadable artifact

Outcome claim

What decision will this dataset or evaluation support, and what would be different if the project succeeds?

Behavior and coverage map

Which capabilities, failure modes, domains, populations, languages, difficulty levels, and edge cases must be represented?

Ground-truth authority

Which judgments are factual, expert, preference-based, policy-based, or legitimately plural - and who has authority to resolve each?

Human-evaluation rubric

Translate broad quality language into observable criteria, anchored examples, scoring rules, and prohibited shortcuts.

Calibration and adjudication

Define reviewer qualification, gold items, disagreement review, drift checks, feedback loops, and rubric-change control.

Acceptance and stop rules

Set acceptance criteria, coverage thresholds, confidence limits, escalation triggers, and the conditions to narrow, redesign, or stop.

03 / Measurement system

Measure whether the evaluation is useful, not merely busy.

Targets belong to discovery; they should not be invented before the task and risk are understood. The scorecard establishes the categories that must be baselined and governed.

Construct validityDoes the rubric measure the intended behavior rather than a proxy?
CoverageAre material domains, difficulties, and failure modes represented?
Judgment qualityAgreement, rationale quality, drift, and adjudication burden.
Decision utilityCan the result support a clear model, data, or product decision?
Operating economicsExpert time, rework, cycle time, and avoidable review load.
Learning velocityHow quickly pilot evidence improves the dataset, rubric, and plan.

05 / The strongest reasonable concern

Can an operating strategist contribute at principal level without a conventional AI-research title sequence?

Test the work pattern, not the label history.

The concern is fair: my verified record does not claim prior ownership of a frontier lab's large-scale LLM evaluation program, and 2017-present represents 9+ years of AI and automation advisory rather than 10+ years under a formal AI title.

The positive hiring case: this role is upstream design, not model implementation. I bring decades of direct experience making quality, risk, human judgment, technical constraints, and scaled execution explicit - plus hands-on work with LLM workflows, RAG patterns, human-in-the-loop design, evaluation, and governance.

The interview should test whether that combination produces sharper discovery, better rubrics, earlier failure-mode detection, and more decision-useful pilots. The hard-objection analysis makes that test concrete.

06 / First 90 days

Prove the advisory system across live discovery, calibration, and pilot decisions.

The first 90 days are organized around contribution, not ceremony: strengthen current programs, codify reusable mechanisms, and leave program managers with better tools.

Days 0-30

Read the portfolio

  • Observe current discovery and client-review practices.
  • Map recurring ambiguity, rework, and evaluation failure modes.
  • Build a common vocabulary for ground truth, coverage, and acceptance.
Days 31-60

Stress-test live designs

  • Apply the Evaluation Design Contract to active or upcoming pilots.
  • Run rubric and edge-case reviews with program, research, and engineering partners.
  • Measure agreement, adjudication burden, coverage, and learning velocity.
Days 61-90

Compound the learning

  • Turn repeated failure modes into reusable design guidance.
  • Create a concise executive review format for pilot readiness.
  • Recommend where to standardize and where domain-specific judgment must remain.

07 / Questions I would ask

Discovery questions that reveal the real design problem.

00
Where do projects most often discover that the original definition of quality was incomplete?
01
Which decisions does the AI lab expect human evaluation to support - model ranking, release readiness, data selection, safety, or something else?
02
How are legitimate expert disagreements represented today rather than averaged away?
03
What separates a successful pilot from a project that produced a large volume of unusable data?
04
Who has final authority to change a rubric after calibration begins?
05
Which coverage gaps have been most expensive: domains, languages, difficulty, adversarial behavior, or rare edge cases?
06
How do program managers currently escalate design risk before contributor operations begin?
07
Where does Braintrust want a reusable standard, and where must the method stay client- or domain-specific?
08
What evidence earns trust in executive and researcher reviews?
09
What would make this strategist indispensable after the first three engagements?

Make the quality bar visible before the project becomes expensive.
