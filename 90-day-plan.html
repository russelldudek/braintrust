First 90 days

Three advisory cycles
Page 1 of 2

Strengthen live work, then codify what repeats.

The plan assumes a remote, flexible advisory role alongside program managers - not direct ownership of contributor operations.

Days 0-30

Read and diagnose

Objectives

  • Observe discovery, solution design, data planning, and client review.
  • Map repeated ambiguity, late design changes, and quality failure modes.
  • Identify decision rights across client, program, research, engineering, contributor, and QA teams.
  • Establish baseline measurement categories without inventing targets.

Outputs

  • Current-state design-risk map
  • Stakeholder and authority matrix
  • Evaluation vocabulary
  • Two candidate engagements for deep review
Days 31-60

Apply and stress-test

Objectives

  • Use the Evaluation Design Contract in active discovery.
  • Facilitate rubric, edge-case, ground-truth, and acceptance reviews.
  • Run calibration with anchored cases and explicit disagreement review.
  • Test whether metrics support a real model, data, or product decision.

Outputs

  • Completed design contracts
  • Judgment Convergence Maps
  • Coverage and edge-case register
  • Pilot-readiness decision memos
Days 61-90

Standardize and transfer

Objectives

  • Separate reusable methods from domain-specific judgment.
  • Convert recurring issues into guidance and examples.
  • Create a compact executive review for risk, evidence, and decisions.
  • Coach program managers to use the mechanisms independently.

Outputs

  • Evaluation-design playbook v1
  • Reusable rubric patterns
  • Executive review cadence
  • Next-quarter improvement backlog

90-day evidence of contribution

Design quality
Fewer unresolved definitions at pilot start; clearer acceptance and stop rules.
Coverage
Explicit high-consequence slices, rare cases, exclusions, and known unknowns.
Judgment system
Observable rubric, calibrated reviewers, explainable disagreement, controlled changes.
Decision utility
Every pilot review concludes with a model/data/product decision and rationale.
Operating economics
Baseline and reduce avoidable rework, late relabeling, redundant review, and unclear escalation.
Capability transfer
Program managers can reuse the contract and review methods without creating bureaucracy.

Risks to manage

Over-standardizing domain judgment; treating agreement as validity; creating rubric complexity evaluators cannot use; allowing client urgency to bypass discovery; hiding ambiguity in averages; designing an advisory process that does not fit program-manager cadence.
