Objection
Russell does not claim prior ownership of a frontier-lab, large-scale LLM evaluation program and does not present 10+ years under formal AI/data-science titles.
Why it matters
The role advises high-stakes project discovery, data strategy, human evaluation, benchmarking, and quality methodology. Shallow familiarity would create more risk than value.
What is verified
9+ years AI/automation advisory and concept development; direct LLM/RAG/agentic workflow, human review, evaluation, and governance patterns; decades of quality systems, high-reliability engineering, scaled operations, risk, standard work, and technical-business translation.
Transfer thesis
The role's deepest requirement is upstream quality architecture: make intent, evidence, human authority, failure modes, calibration, and decisions explicit before scaled execution.
What remains to validate
Depth with current frontier-evaluation techniques, dataset program conventions, client-specific research workflows, and the speed at which Russell can absorb the lab's technical context.

Why the nontraditional path may be useful

A principal advisor must challenge assumptions across research, engineering, program management, contributors, QA, and executives. Russell's evidence spans those operating boundaries rather than one narrow functional lane. The benefit is practical systems judgment; the risk is domain-specific evaluation depth. Both can be tested directly.

Interview tests that resolve the concern

Page 2 of 2

Discovery test

Give Russell an ambiguous model-quality goal. Evaluate whether he identifies the decision, stakeholders, risk, behavior, unknowns, and design questions before proposing data volume.

Rubric test

Ask him to operationalize a broad criterion such as helpfulness. Evaluate observability, adjacent-score anchors, rationale requirements, and proxy-risk detection.

Coverage test

Provide a candidate dataset. Evaluate whether he finds missing domains, difficulties, adversarial cases, ambiguity, and high-consequence slices.

Calibration test

Show conflicting reviewer rationales. Evaluate whether he distinguishes reviewer error, rubric defect, legitimate plural judgment, and adjudication need.

Pilot-decision test

Provide mixed metrics. Evaluate whether he makes an explicit advance, narrow, redesign, or hold recommendation with evidence and uncertainty.

Executive communication test

Ask for a two-minute project review. Evaluate whether he makes risk, evidence, tradeoffs, and next decision clear without hiding behind methodology.

Positive hiring case

Hire Russell if the work sample demonstrates principal judgment: stronger questions, earlier risk detection, clearer human authority, a usable quality rubric, a defensible pilot decision, and communication that improves both research and program execution. Do not hire on title equivalence alone - positive or negative.

90-day de-risking mechanism

Pair initial engagements with an experienced program manager and relevant research/engineering stakeholders; review two live project designs; baseline changes in unresolved definitions, coverage gaps, adjudication burden, rework, and decision clarity; codify only what repeats.
